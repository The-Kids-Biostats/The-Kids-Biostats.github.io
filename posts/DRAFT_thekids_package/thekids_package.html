Overview

A major attraction of the R programming language is its vast library of free packages, which can be found on both CRAN (the Comprehensive R Archive Network) and hosted on other sites such as GitHub. These packages can provide ready-made functions, datasets and/or templates for sometimes staggeringly niche tasks. In the field of statistics and data analysis, it is rare to encounter any methodology or task which has not also been implemented in an R package.

R packages can be easily developed for use by individuals or teams, to automate or simplify common tasks. This is the purpose of thekidsbiostats for our teams. Its two primary purposes can be broadly described as providing:

  1. Functions to automate and or simplify common tasks in our workflow.
  2. Functions and templates which serve to apply consistent formatting and theming to documents, ggplots, tables, etc.

This post will provide examples of our most frequently used functions. While some of these may seem at first glance to be specific to The Kids Research Institute Australia (TKRIA), they should be readily adjusted for use in other organisations with tweaks to fonts, colours, templates etc.

The package (and all of its code!) are available here: https://github.com/The-Kids-Biostats/thekidsbiostats

Conclusions

Overall, the gtsummary::tbl_summary is the best summary table function out of those explored in this blog. In general, the range of in-built parameters and helper functions allows for the greatest flexibility in producing a relatively publication-ready summary table.
